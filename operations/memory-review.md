# Decision, Experiment, and Memory Review

## Decision records

Use a decision record for consequential or reusable choices. Capture:

- context and objective
- chosen action and alternatives
- rationale, evidence, assumptions, and confidence
- boundary type and expected side effects
- reversibility and proof required to verify rollback
- review date, outcome, and lesson

Outcome rubric:

- `win` — expected outcome achieved with acceptable risk
- `partial` — mixed result; retain only after refinement
- `miss` — failed outcome or unacceptable risk; add a guardrail

## Experiments

Every experiment needs a falsifiable hypothesis, smallest safe test, primary
metric, review time, and a final result of `won`, `lost`, or `inconclusive`.

Do not promote a rule from one result. Promote repeated or high-impact lessons
through the self-improving memory process.

## Memory review

Run a quick review after major work and a fuller review periodically:

1. Freshness — stale open items need review or archival.
2. Contradictions — newer explicit decisions override older notes.
3. Bloat — merge duplicates and archive one-off rules.
4. Actionability — retained memory must affect behavior or decisions.
5. Outcome linkage — important decisions need actual outcomes.
6. Privacy — remove unnecessary personal or credential-like data.

Prefer a compact set of active rules. Historical evidence belongs in an archive,
not in the hot operating set.
