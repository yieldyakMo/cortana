# Indicator Guide

Indicators summarize historical price and volume. They do not predict outcomes.

## Trend

- SMA and EMA help describe trend direction and potential dynamic levels.
- Crossovers are lagging confirmations, not standalone entries.

## Momentum

- RSI describes recent gain/loss balance. Overbought and oversold conditions can
  persist during strong trends.
- MACD compares fast and slow EMAs; the signal line is an EMA of MACD history.

## Volatility

- Bollinger Bands describe dispersion around a moving average.
- ATR measures movement magnitude, not direction.

## Volume and levels

- Volume can strengthen or weaken a price interpretation.
- Support and resistance are zones, not guarantees.
- Order-book walls can move or disappear and may be deceptive.

## Minimum research discipline

- Use multiple timeframes.
- Confirm data timestamps and source.
- Distinguish closed candles from incomplete candles.
- Avoid single-indicator conclusions.
- State what would invalidate the interpretation.
